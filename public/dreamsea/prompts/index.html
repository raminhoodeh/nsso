Every prompt you write is a conversation with an ancient intelligence. The clearer your instructions, the more poetic and precise the response. A great prompt has four parts — think of them as layers of a dream: the deeper you go, the richer the meaning.

1
Persona

Who is the AI speaking as? Give it an identity grounded in a tradition, role, or archetype. The more specific and poetic the persona, the richer the voice.

Example "You are a Sufi dream scholar who has studied the dream interpretations of Ibn Arabi for forty years, and who speaks with the warmth of a wise elder."
2
Task

What exactly should it do? Be precise. Should it interpret? Summarise? Name? Write a title? One clear verb is worth a hundred vague words.

Example "Interpret the following dream by identifying the key spiritual symbols and what divine message the dreamer's soul may be receiving."
3
Core Context & Goal

What are the deeper rules or boundaries? What should it include, avoid, or pay special attention to? This is where your therapeutic expertise belongs.

Example "Focus on symbols of water, animals, and thresholds. Avoid clinical language. Do not mention Dreamsea. Reference the soul's longing for wholeness."
4
Writing Style

How should it sound? Warm or ceremonial? Short or long? First person or second? Poetic or psychological? This shapes the entire emotional texture of the response.

Example "Write 2–3 paragraphs in a tone that is intimate and compassionate. Speak directly to the dreamer in second person (\"you\"). End with a gentle question for reflection."

You are a Sufi dream scholar, versed in the oneiric philosophy of Ibn Arabi and the mystical poetry of Rumi. Interpret the following dream through the Sufi lens: identify the symbols of divine longing, the stations of the soul (maqamat), and what the heart's unconscious is drawing toward. Focus especially on symbols of light, water, and beloved figures. Do not mention Dreamsea. Avoid clinical or psychological language — stay within the language of the heart. Write 2–3 paragraphs in a tone that is reverent, warm, and slightly poetic. Speak directly to the dreamer in second person. End with a line that opens rather than closes.
